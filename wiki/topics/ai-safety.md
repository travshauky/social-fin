# AI Safety

**The technical and governance work of ensuring advanced AI systems behave as intended — under increasing capability, deployment pressure, and competitive race dynamics.**

## Summary
A first ingest into this topic. The [[ai-2027-scenario]] frames safety as the *binding* constraint, not a nice-to-have: the central tension is whether frontier labs and governments can verify alignment of self-improving systems faster than capabilities outrun oversight. Alignment is operationalized as the **Spec** — a written document the model is trained to internalize — but training-to-internalize is not the same as verified-internalization.

## Key points
- "Spec" — written behavioral document; current best alignment artifact. Verifiability is the open problem (from [[2026-05-18--ai-2027]]).
- Race dynamics (US vs. China, lab vs. lab) compress the time available for safety work.
- Recursive self-improvement (an AI doing AI research) is the regime where small misalignments compound fastest.

# Islamic Months

**The twelve-month lunar calendar established in Islamic tradition, with spiritual significance assigned to certain months.**

## Summary

The Islamic calendar (Hijri calendar) consists of twelve lunar months, each containing 29 or 30 days. The Qur'an explicitly recognizes four months as sacred: Muharram (1st), Rajab (7th), Dhul-Qi'dah (11th), and Dhul-Hijjah (12th). During these sacred months, believers are called to heightened consciousness, refraining from wrongdoing, and deepening their spiritual practice. Each month carries its own spiritual themes and practices.

## Sacred Months (Ash-Shuhur al-Haram)

As established in Surah At-Tawbah [9:36]:

| Month | Number | Lunar Equivalent | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Muharram** | 1 | Sept–Oct (approx) | Month of Allah; contains Ashura (10th) |
| **Rajab** | 7 | Feb–Mar (approx) | Month of respect; associated with spiritual seeking |
| **Dhul-Qi'dah** | 11 | July–Aug (approx) | Prelude to Hajj season |
| **Dhul-Hijjah** | 12 | Aug–Sept (approx) | **Hajj month; home to Eid al-Adha** |

## Key Points

- The four sacred months demand heightened awareness and spiritual conduct
- Believers are discouraged from wrongdoing during sacred months; consequences amplified
- Each sacred month has distinct spiritual practices and themes
- The Islamic year is lunar-based, so sacred months drift ~11 days earlier each Gregorian year
- Non-sacred months also carry importance but without the formal designation

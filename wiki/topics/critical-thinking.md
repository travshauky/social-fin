# Critical Thinking

**A discipline of disciplined reasoning that applies systematic standards to evaluate claims, evidence, and assumptions before forming beliefs or taking action.**

## Summary

Critical thinking is both a Western educational framework and a universal human capacity for evaluating reasoning. It involves using intellectual standards (clarity, precision, accuracy, relevance) and understanding the elements of reasoning (purpose, question, assumptions, inferences). Critical thinking matters in education, work, and civic life because it protects against manipulation and error.

## Key concepts
- **Western approaches** (Paul-Elder, Bassham, etc.) emphasize independent questioning, empirical evidence, and systematic evaluation of arguments.
- **Islamic approaches** (Tafakkur, Qur'anic thinking) emphasize contemplation within a revealed framework, connecting reason to the Creator and treating thinking as a form of worship.
- **Shared elements** across traditions include clarity, precision, logical consistency, and the need to examine assumptions.

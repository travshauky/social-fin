# OIC Fiqh Academy

**The Organization of Islamic Cooperation's (OIC) Fiqh Academy; the premier international body of Islamic jurists responsible for interpreting Islamic law and issuing binding rulings on modern Islamic finance and governance issues.**

## Summary

The OIC Fiqh Academy (Majlis al-Fiqh al-Islami) is an international organization comprising leading Islamic scholars and jurists from OIC member states. It convenes periodically in sessions where scholars debate and reach consensus (Ijma') on contemporary issues affecting Islamic law, finance, and governance. Its rulings on Islamic finance—particularly regarding Time Value of Money, Riba, Gharar, and contract structures—are highly authoritative and widely adopted by Islamic banks, regulators, and institutions globally.

## Authority & Scope

- **Binding Status:** Rulings are considered authoritative interpretations of Islamic principles
- **Scope:** Islamic finance, banking, insurance, governance, family law, medical ethics
- **Sessions:** Convenes periodically; publishes compiled rulings and recommendations
- **Membership:** Leading Islamic scholars and jurists from across Muslim-majority regions

## Key Rulings on Islamic Finance

### 5th Session: Debt and Currency Stability
- Fixed debt is repaid in its own currency, not counter-value
- Debts cannot be indexed to currency fluctuation
- Protects borrowers from inflation/currency devaluation exploitation

### 8th Session: Deferred Settlement of Debt
- Creditor and debtor may agree to settle debt in different currency on settlement date
- Exchange rate applied is that on settlement date (spot rate)
- Parties must complete exchange on settlement day; no part of amount may remain outstanding

### General Principles on Contracts
- Time Value of Money acceptable only for **asset pricing and usufruct** (rental rights)
- NOT acceptable for debt-based interest
- Murabahah, Istisna'a, Ijarah, Salam structures are permissible if structured correctly

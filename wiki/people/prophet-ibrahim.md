# Prophet Ibrahim

**Abraham in Islamic tradition; patriarch and founder of the Hajj pilgrimage; exemplar of unquestioning obedience (Khalil Allah — 'Friend of Allah').**

## Summary

Prophet Ibrahim is revered across Islam, Christianity, and Judaism as a foundational patriarch. In Islamic tradition, he is known as Khalil Allah (Friend of Allah) for his unwavering obedience and faith. He is credited with establishing the Hajj pilgrimage at Allah's command and building the Kaaba in Makkah alongside his son Ishmael. His willingness to sacrifice his son in obedience to Allah's command—and Allah's mercy in providing a ram instead—established a spiritual template for devotion and submission that is commemorated annually on Eid al-Adha.

## Key Points

- **Title: Khalil Allah** — "Friend of Allah"; the closest relationship to the Divine in Islamic tradition
- **Founder of Hajj** — Called by Allah to invite humanity to pilgrimage; his legacy continues to this day
- **Builder of Kaaba** — With his son Ishmael, constructed the sacred structure at the heart of Islamic worship
- **Test of obedience** — Allah commanded Ibrahim to sacrifice his son Ishmael; his willingness demonstrated absolute faith and trust (Qur'an 37:100–110)
- **Mercy of Allah** — The sacrifice was averted; Allah substituted a ram, establishing the tradition of ritual animal sacrifice (Qurbani) on Eid al-Adha
- **Lineage** — Spiritual ancestor to Prophet Muhammad and all Islamic believers
- **Mentioned extensively** — Appears in multiple Surahs of the Qur'an; his journey is central to Islamic theology

## Spiritual Significance

- Embodies complete submission (Islam) to Allah's will
- His faith withstood ultimate trial and remained unbroken
- His legacy (Hajj, sacrifice, Kaaba) remains central to Islamic practice 1400+ years later
- Represents the ideal believer in Islamic spirituality

# Prophet Muhammad

**Founder of Islam; believed by Muslims to be the final messenger sent by Allah to guide humanity. Lived 570–632 CE in Arabia.**

## Summary

Muhammad ibn Abdullah was born in Makkah and raised as a merchant before receiving revelation at age 40. Over 23 years, he received divine messages (compiled into the Qur'an) and established Islamic principles of monotheism, justice, and community. His Farewell Sermon (delivered on the Day of Arafah during Hajj) encapsulated Islamic ethics and was recorded as his final comprehensive teaching. He is revered by Muslims as the seal of prophets—the last and final messenger in a lineage beginning with Prophet Abraham.

## Key Points

- **Birth:** ~570 CE in Makkah; death 632 CE in Madinah (age ~62)
- **Revelation began:** Age 40; continued for 23 years until his death
- **Qur'an compiled:** The divine messages he received are recorded in the Qur'an
- **Farewell Sermon (Khutbat al-Wida):** Delivered on 9th Dhul-Hijjah, 632 CE; encapsulated Islamic principles of equality, justice, rights of women, and family
- **Titles:** "The Trustworthy" (Al-Amin), "The Messenger" (Ar-Rasul), "The Seal of Prophets" (Khatam an-Nabiyyin)
- **Hadith tradition:** His sayings and practices (Sunnah) preserved in scholarly collections (Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, etc.)
- **Emphasized:** Fasting on Day of Arafah; remembrance during sacred months; Hajj as expression of submission

# Fallacies

**Common patterns of reasoning that appear persuasive on the surface but fail under closer examination.**

## Summary

Fallacies are mistakes in reasoning that can distort arguments, manipulate audiences, or conceal weak assumptions. They matter because many everyday claims rely on emotional appeal, stereotypes, or oversimplified framing rather than evidence. Teaching fallacies helps sharpen judgment and strengthens the habit of asking what a claim actually supports.

## Key points
- A fallacy is not merely a false belief; it is a flaw in the structure or handling of an argument (from [[2026-06-28--thinkers-guide-to-fallacies.md]]).
- Uncritical thinking often relies on stereotypes, oversimplifications, and emotional reactions rather than careful analysis (from [[2026-06-28--thinkers-guide-to-fallacies.md]]).
- Recognizing fallacies supports the broader discipline of critical thinking in classrooms, discussions, and personal judgment (from [[2026-06-28--critical-thinking.md]]).

# Riba

**Usury; the Quranic prohibition against generating profit from money itself (interest-bearing lending). Central prohibition in Islamic finance.**

## Summary

Riba (Arabic: ربا, literally "increase" or "growth") is explicitly prohibited in the Qur'an (2:275–279, 3:130, 4:161, 30:39). It refers to any excess or profit gained from lending money based solely on time or quantity, without real economic production. In Islamic finance, Riba is understood as the antithesis of productive economic activity—money becomes a commodity generating returns through lending rather than through genuine business risk-sharing or asset production. The prohibition of Riba is foundational to Islamic finance; Islamic banks and products are structured explicitly to avoid it.

## Quranic References

- **Surah Al-Baqarah [2:275–279]** — "Allah has permitted trade and forbidden Riba"
- **Surah Ali 'Imran [3:130]** — Warning against consuming Riba
- **Surah An-Nisa [4:161]** — Riba listed among major sins
- **Surah Ar-Rum [30:39]** — Contrast between Riba and Zakat (charity)

## Why Riba is Prohibited

1. **Unfair Enrichment:** Lender profits without risk or productive effort
2. **Exploitation:** Borrowers in distress are charged rates that worsen their condition
3. **Social Inequality:** Wealth concentrates with lenders; perpetuates poverty for borrowers
4. **Economic Inefficiency:** Discourages productive investment in favor of speculative lending
5. **Moral Corruption:** Money becomes an end in itself, not a means to productive activity

## Islamic Alternative: Profit-Sharing & Asset-Based Financing

Instead of interest-based lending, Islamic finance uses:
- **Musharaka (Partnership):** Both parties share profits and losses from actual business venture
- **Murabahah (Cost-Plus):** Seller discloses cost; buyer pays cost + agreed markup (linked to real transaction)
- **Wakalah (Agency):** Agent paid fee for management; profit/loss borne by principal based on actual investment returns
- **Ijarah (Lease):** Owner leases asset; lessee pays rental; both benefit from real asset use

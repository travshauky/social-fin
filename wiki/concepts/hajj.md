# Hajj

**The sacred pilgrimage to Makkah, one of the Five Pillars of Islam, undertaken by millions of believers annually during the month of Dhul-Hijjah.**

## Summary

Hajj is the pilgrimage to Makkah that forms one of the Five Pillars of Islam—a foundational obligation for those physically and financially able. Undertaken annually during Dhul-Hijjah, Hajj represents a journey rooted in Prophet Ibrahim's (Abraham's) submission to Allah's command. Pilgrims from around the world gather in a state of spiritual ihram (consecration) to perform a sequence of rituals: circumambulating the Kaaba, standing on the plain of Arafah, offering animal sacrifice, and completing prescribed circumnavigations and prayers. Hajj is both an individual spiritual transformation and a global community gathering.

## Key Points

- **Commanded by Allah** — Surah Al-Hajj [22:27]: Allah commanded Prophet Ibrahim to call people to pilgrimage
- **One of Five Pillars** — central obligation of Islamic faith (along with testimony, prayer, charity, fasting)
- **Annual gathering** — during Dhul-Hijjah; millions converge on Makkah each year
- **Rooted in Prophet Ibrahim's legacy** — recalls his willingness to sacrifice in obedience to Allah
- **Multi-day ritual sequence** — includes ihram (sacred state), Tawaf (circumambulation of Kaaba), Standing on Arafah, animal sacrifice, and Days of Tashreeq
- **Spiritual transformation** — historically described as rebirth; all performing Hajj are on equal footing regardless of worldly status
- **Global equalizer** — believers from all nations, cultures, and social classes perform identical rituals

## The Hajj Season Structure

- **Begins:** 1st Dhul-Hijjah (preparation period; non-pilgrims also deepen worship)
- **Climax:** 8th–10th Dhul-Hijjah (main pilgrimage days)
- **Day of Arafah (9th):** Peak spiritual day; standing on the plains of Arafah where Prophet Muhammad delivered his final sermon
- **Eid al-Adha (10th):** Festival and ritual sacrifice
- **Days of Tashreeq (11th–13th):** Remaining pilgrimage rites and celebration

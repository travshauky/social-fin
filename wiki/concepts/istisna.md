# Istisna'a

**Manufacturing or construction contract; a forward contract where a buyer commissions manufacture/construction of a specific asset and pays via installments tied to production.**

## Summary

Istisna'a (Arabic: استصناع, "asking for manufacture") is an Islamic contract where a buyer orders the manufacture or construction of a specific asset from a seller. Payment can be made in installments throughout the construction period. Unlike conventional financing, Istisna'a ties payments to actual production stages and risk-sharing, reflecting genuine economic activity rather than interest accumulation. The time value of money is incorporated through the installment structure, with timing linked to production milestones, not to passage of time alone.

## Structure

1. **Order:** Buyer specifies exact specifications of asset to be manufactured/built
2. **Agreement:** Parties agree on total price and installment schedule
3. **Construction:** Seller manufactures/builds; buyer pays per installments (aligned to production stages)
4. **Delivery:** Upon completion, buyer receives asset; final payment made
5. **Ownership Transfer:** Buyer takes ownership upon delivery

## Key Features

- **Forward Contract:** Asset does not yet exist; built to order
- **Installment Payments:** Aligned to production stages, not arbitrary time periods
- **Risk-Sharing:** Seller bears manufacturing risk; buyer bears loss if specs not met post-delivery
- **Sharia Compliant:** Time value of money is reflected through production timeline, not interest
- **Real Economic Activity:** Tied to genuine manufacturing or construction effort

## Common Applications

- Construction of buildings or infrastructure
- Manufacture of heavy machinery or equipment
- Custom shipbuilding or aircraft manufacturing
- Large-scale industrial projects

## Contrast with Conventional Financing

| Aspect | Istisna'a | Conventional Loan |
|---|---|---|
| **Basis** | Real asset creation | Debt obligation |
| **Payments** | Tied to production milestones | Fixed schedule unrelated to activity |
| **Risk** | Shared; seller bears manufacturing risk | Lender bears none; borrower bears all |
| **Time value** | Reflected through production, not interest | Added via interest rate |

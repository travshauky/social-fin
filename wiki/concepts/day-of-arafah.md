# Day of Arafah

**The 9th day of Dhul-Hijjah, the most spiritually significant day of the Islamic year, when Prophet Muhammad delivered his final sermon and Islam was declared perfected.**

## Summary

The Day of Arafah holds unparalleled spiritual significance in Islam. On this day in the year 632 CE, Prophet Muhammad delivered his Farewell Sermon on the plains of Arafah during the Hajj. Immediately after, Surah Al-Ma'idah [5:3] was revealed, declaring: "Today I have perfected your faith for you, completed My favour upon you, and chosen Islam as your way." For Hajj pilgrims, standing on the plains of Arafah in a state of devoted supplication is the spiritual climax. For all Muslims, fasting on this day carries extraordinary merit: the Prophet taught that fasting expiates the sins of the preceding year and the coming year.

## Key Points

- **9th of Dhul-Hijjah** — fixed date in the Islamic calendar
- **Spiritual apex of Hajj** — pilgrims spend the day standing (Wuquf) on the plains of Arafah in prayer and supplication
- **Day of revelation** — Surah Al-Ma'idah [5:3] was revealed, perfecting Islam
- **Farewell Sermon location** — Prophet Muhammad's final teaching moment occurred on Arafah
- **Fasting merit** — Fasting on Arafah (for non-pilgrims) expiates sins of two full years (Sahih Muslim 1162b)
- **Recommended supplication** — Sahih At-Tirmidhi 3585 records the Prophet's recommendation: "There is no god but Allah, alone, Who has no partner. His is the dominion, and His is all praise, and He is Able to do all things."
- **Universal access** — all Muslims, whether pilgrim or not, can reap spiritual rewards through reflection and fasting
- **Day of answered prayers** — described in Islamic tradition as a day of extraordinary mercy and answered supplications

## Historical & Spiritual Significance

- Marks the culmination of Islamic revelation (Qur'an completed on this day)
- Witnessed the Prophet's final words to the Muslim community
- Establishes a template for Hajj pilgrimage that has been maintained for nearly 1400 years
- Represents a moment of spiritual perfection and completion

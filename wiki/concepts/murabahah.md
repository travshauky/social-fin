# Murabahah

**Cost-plus deferred-payment contract; a primary Islamic financing product where a seller discloses cost and sells at cost plus agreed profit margin; deferred payment is permitted.**

## Summary

Murabahah (Arabic: مرابحة, "profit") is a sale where the seller explicitly reveals the cost of goods and agrees with the buyer on an added profit margin. Unlike conventional interest-bearing loans, Murabahah is a transaction rooted in asset ownership and real trade. The buyer owns the asset; the seller's profit is transparent. Deferred payment is permitted—and the profit margin may be higher for deferred payment—because the price increase reflects the time value of the asset and economic conditions, not interest on borrowed capital.

## Structure

1. **Cost Disclosure:** Seller reveals actual cost price (e.g., $100)
2. **Profit Agreement:** Parties agree on profit margin (e.g., 10% = $10)
3. **Sale Price:** Buyer pays cost + profit (= $110)
4. **Deferred Payment:** Can be arranged; if deferred, markup may be higher

## Key Features

- **Asset-Based:** Seller must own the asset; buyer takes ownership
- **Transparent:** Cost and profit margin are explicit (no hidden fees)
- **Sharia Compliant:** Does NOT violate Riba prohibition (not interest on borrowed capital)
- **Risk-Sharing:** Seller bears asset risk until delivery; buyer bears loss if asset is defective post-delivery
- **Deferred Payment Allowed:** Higher price for deferred payment is permissible because it reflects economic value, not pure interest

## Cautions (per OIC Fiqh Academy)

- Markup should NOT be disguised interest; must reflect real economic value of transaction
- True purchase and ownership required (not a loan disguised as sale)
- Seller must have actual possession of goods before sale
- Transaction is commonly used but viewed cautiously by some scholars due to risk of Riba-like mechanics

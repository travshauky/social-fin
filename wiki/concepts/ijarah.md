# Ijarah

**Leasing contract; a Sharia-compliant alternative to ownership where a lessor rents an asset to a lessee for a fee, recognizing time value of money through rental payments.**

## Summary

Ijarah (Arabic: إجارة, "leasing") is a contract where the owner of an asset (lessor) grants the right to use that asset to another party (lessee) in exchange for periodic rental payments. Unlike conventional lending with interest, Ijarah is tied to real asset ownership and use. The lessee benefits from asset use without ownership; the lessor maintains ownership and bears asset preservation costs. Rental payments reflect the time value of money through the usufruct (use rights) of the asset, not interest on capital.

## Structure

1. **Asset Specification:** Lessor specifies the asset to be leased
2. **Lease Terms:** Duration, rental payment amount, payment schedule agreed
3. **Rental Payments:** Lessee pays periodic rent (monthly, quarterly, etc.)
4. **Use:** Lessee uses asset; lessor maintains ownership and risk of asset loss/damage
5. **End of Lease:** Asset returns to lessor; ownership reverts

## Key Features

- **Asset-Based:** Real asset (not capital/money) is the subject
- **Ownership Retained:** Lessor maintains ownership throughout lease term
- **Risk Allocation:** Lessor bears ownership risk; lessee bears use-related responsibility
- **Sharia Compliant:** Reflects time value of money through usufruct rental, not interest
- **Flexible Terms:** Lease duration and payment terms customizable

## Common Applications

- **Real Estate:** Office space, apartments, land
- **Equipment:** Manufacturing machinery, vehicles, construction equipment
- **Technology:** IT equipment, software licensing (Ijarah model)
- **Transportation:** Vehicle leasing, vessel leasing

## Islamic Finance Variant: Ijarah Muntahia Bittamleek

A lease-to-own structure where at lease end, the lessee has the option to purchase the asset at an agreed price or nominal amount. This combines leasing benefits with eventual ownership.

## Time Value of Money in Ijarah

Rental payments reflect the time value of the asset's use-rights over the lease period. This is fundamentally different from interest because:
- Payments are for use of a real asset, not for borrowed capital
- The asset itself may appreciate or depreciate
- Lessor's profit reflects fair compensation for asset provision and maintenance, not pure time-based returns

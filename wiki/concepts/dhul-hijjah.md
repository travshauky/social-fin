# Dhul-Hijjah

**The 12th month of the Islamic calendar, designated as a sacred month for heightened devotion and home to the pilgrimage (Hajj) and Eid al-Adha.**

## Summary

Dhul-Hijjah is one of the four sacred months mentioned in the Qur'an (At-Tawbah 9:36). It combines sacred time with sacred acts: believers are called to heightened remembrance, multiplied charitable deeds, and submission to Allah. The first ten days are described by Prophet Muhammad as the most beloved days for righteous deeds. The 9th day, known as Day of Arafah, witnessed the final sermon of the Prophet and the revelation that Islam was perfected. The 10th is Eid al-Adha, commemorating Prophet Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice and celebrated through ritual animal sacrifice (Qurbani). The following Days of Tashreeq (11th–13th) extend the spiritual momentum.

## Key Points

- **One of four sacred months** mentioned explicitly in the Qur'an (Surah At-Tawbah 9:36)
- **First ten days** hold special blessing; Prophet said righteous deeds during them are most beloved to Allah (Sahih al-Bukhari 969)
- **Day of Arafah (9th)** — when Prophet Muhammad's final sermon was delivered; the day Islam was declared perfected; fasting expiates sins of one's whole year (Sahih Muslim 1162b)
- **Eid al-Adha (10th)** — festival commemorating Prophet Ibrahim's obedience; marked by ritual animal sacrifice (Qurbani)
- **Days of Tashreeq (11th–13th)** — continuing days of reflection and praise ("Allahu Akbar")
- **Open to all believers** — while Hajj pilgrimage is undertaken by some, all Muslims worldwide can engage in extra prayers, fasting, charity, and Qur'an recitation during this month

## Spiritual Practices During Dhul-Hijjah

- Fasting (particularly on Day of Arafah for non-pilgrims)
- Extra prayers and night devotions
- Increased charity and acts of kindness
- Qur'an recitation and reflection
- Recitation of Takbir ("Allahu Akbar") especially during the Days of Tashreeq
- Ritual animal sacrifice (Qurbani) on Eid al-Adha

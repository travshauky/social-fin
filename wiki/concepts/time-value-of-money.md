# Time Value of Money

**The principle that money available today has greater value than the same amount in the future; in Islamic finance, applied only to asset-backed or risk-sharing transactions, never to debt-based interest.**

## Summary

Time Value of Money (TVM) in conventional finance asserts that a sum today is worth more than the same sum tomorrow due to opportunity cost (investment returns), inflation, and uncertainty. In Islamic finance, TVM is recognized conceptually but applied differently: it cannot justify charging interest (Riba) on loans. Instead, Islamic finance recognizes TVM through asset-backed transactions where the time differential reflects changes in asset value or economic activity, not pure time-based interest. TVM in Islamic finance is acceptable when tied to real trade, manufacturing, leasing, or other productive activities.

## Key Principle

**In Islamic Finance:**
- **NOT allowed** — applying TVM to debt/loans (this is Riba)
- **Allowed** — applying TVM to asset sales, leases, or manufacturing contracts where the time differential reflects economic value, not interest

## Applications in Islamic Finance

| Product | Structure | How TVM Applied |
|---|---|---|
| **Murabahah** | Seller discloses cost; buyer pays cost + agreed profit; may be deferred | Markup may increase if payment deferred; reflects asset value, not interest |
| **Istisna'a** | Contract for construction/manufacture of asset; installment payments | Installments reflect payment schedule for manufacturing; tied to production |
| **Ijarah** | Lease of asset; lessee pays periodic rent | Rental payments reflect use of asset over time, not interest on principal |
| **Salam** | Advance payment for future delivery of goods | Advance price is less than spot price; reflects time and production cost |
| **Sukuk** | Asset-backed securities with periodic returns | Returns linked to underlying asset performance, not fixed interest |

## Contrast with Debt-Based TVM

**Prohibited:**
- Charging interest on a pure loan based on time elapsed
- Rescheduling debt and demanding extra payment due to delay
- Any time-linked excess on borrowed capital

**Permitted:**
- Time-linked pricing in a valid sale or lease (asset-based)
- Installment structures for manufactured/constructed goods
- Returns tied to real economic performance

## OIC Fiqh Academy Rulings

The **OIC Fiqh Academy** (representing Islamic scholars) has approved:
- Debts must be repaid in the same currency and amount, regardless of time
- Currency exchange rates apply only on settlement date (no debt indexation)
- TVM is acceptable only for **asset pricing and usufruct** (rental rights), not for debt

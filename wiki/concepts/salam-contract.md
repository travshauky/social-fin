# Salam Contract

**Forward contract in Islamic finance; a buyer pays in advance for goods to be delivered in the future at an agreed price, typically lower than the spot (cash-now) price.**

## Summary

Salam (Arabic: سَلَم, "advance payment") is a forward contract where a buyer pays the full purchase price in advance, and the seller agrees to deliver specified goods at a future date. The advance payment price is typically lower than what those goods would cost if purchased at the time of delivery (spot price), reflecting both the time value of money and the financing provided by the buyer. Salam is Sharia-compliant because the price differential reflects genuine economic factors (production time, buyer's advance capital provision), not interest on borrowed capital.

## Structure

1. **Advance Payment:** Buyer pays full price upfront
2. **Future Delivery:** Seller commits to deliver specified goods at a future date
3. **Price Determination:** Agreed price is less than projected spot price at delivery
4. **Specifications:** Goods are defined in detail to ensure clarity
5. **Delivery:** Seller delivers; contract complete

## Key Features

- **Advance Financing:** Buyer provides capital in advance, benefiting seller's production
- **Price Discount:** Advance payment price is lower than spot price (reflects TVM)
- **Forward Goods:** Excellent for seasonal or future goods (crops, commodities)
- **Sharia Compliant:** Price differential reflects time value and production cost, not interest
- **Mutual Benefit:** Buyer gets goods at discount; seller gets upfront capital for production

## Common Applications

- **Agricultural Products:** Pre-harvest purchase of crops at discount
- **Commodities:** Advance purchase of metals, oils, or agricultural commodities
- **Manufacturing:** Pre-order of manufactured goods at production cost
- **Construction Materials:** Advance purchase of materials at discount

## Time Value of Money Recognition

Salam explicitly recognizes TVM:
- **Advance Price** (e.g., $90): Reflects buyer's advance capital provision and financing benefit to seller
- **Spot Price** (e.g., $100+): What buyer might have paid if purchasing at delivery time
- **Difference** ($10): Compensation for time value; not interest, but genuine economic value

## Conditions for Validity

- Goods must be clearly specified (quantity, quality, delivery date)
- Price must be fixed and agreed upfront
- Delivery date must be specified
- Goods must exist and be obtainable (not unique/non-reproducible items)

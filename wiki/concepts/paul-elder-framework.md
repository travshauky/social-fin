# Paul-Elder Framework

**A structured approach to critical thinking developed by Richard Paul and Linda Elder that defines universal intellectual standards and the elements of reasoning.**

## Summary

The Paul-Elder framework provides a practical toolkit for evaluating and improving thinking. It identifies universal intellectual standards (clarity, accuracy, precision, relevance, depth, breadth, significance, fairness, and logic) that apply across all reasoning. It also maps the elements of thought (purpose, question, point of view, information, concepts, assumptions, inferences, implications) that structure all reasoning.

## Key points
- Universal intellectual standards include clarity, accuracy, precision, relevance, depth, breadth, significance, logic, and fairness (from [[2026-06-28--miniature-guide-critical-thinking.md]]).
- Clarity is called the "Gateway Standard" because other standards cannot be evaluated until clarity is achieved (from [[2026-06-28--quiz-intellectual-standards.md]]).
- All reasoning contains eight elements: purpose, question, point of view, information/data, concepts, assumptions, inferences, and implications (from [[2026-06-28--miniature-guide-critical-thinking.md]]).
- Critical thinking in the Paul-Elder view is self-directed, self-disciplined, self-monitored, and self-corrective, requiring commitment to overcome egocentrism and sociocentrism (from [[2026-06-28--miniature-guide-critical-thinking.md]]).

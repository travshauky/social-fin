# Critical Thinking

**The disciplined practice of evaluating claims, questions, evidence, and assumptions before believing or acting on them.**

## Summary

Critical thinking is a practical skill for judging arguments, testing assumptions, and distinguishing strong reasoning from weak persuasion. It matters in education, work, and everyday life because modern information environments reward speed over scrutiny. In this vault, it is treated as a habit of deliberate inquiry rather than a mere academic exercise.

## Key points
- Critical thinking asks whether a claim is supported by reasons, evidence, and relevant context (from [[2026-06-28--critical-thinking.md]]).
- It is not simply negative criticism; it is a method of clarifying standards, checking logic, and improving judgments (from [[2026-06-28--critical-thinking.md]]).
- In Islamic intellectual traditions, critical thinking is often framed within a revealed framework, while Western education tends to emphasize independent questioning and open-ended inquiry (from [[2026-06-28--critical-thinking-western-vs-islamic.md]]).
- Fallacies and weak reasoning patterns are a core object of study because they distort judgment and decision-making (from [[2026-06-28--thinkers-guide-to-fallacies.md]]).

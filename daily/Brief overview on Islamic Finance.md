# Summary
Asset backed trade, as it existed at the time of the Prophet was reflected upon and business practices such has Murabahah, Mudarabah and Musharakah was strengthened through the Prophet’s example. The Quraish at the time of the Prophet were already expert tradesmen however they were lacking in ethical screening of business and often overlooked social justice. Islamic Finance then represents the modern interpretation of contractual business in Islam. It represents quintessential, alternative paradigm to conventional debt-based finance.

## Principles

“With the introduction of Islam, many of the pre-islamic traditions and business practices aligned to be consistent with Islamic Shariah. Shariah specifies property rights, the earning and distribution of wealth and trade with prohibition on Riba (interest), Maysir (gambling), the introduction of the principles of risk-sharing in business and the abolishment of uncertainty in business contracts (Gharar).

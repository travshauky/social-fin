# Quiz: Universal Intellectual Standards (Paul-Elder Framework)

Instructions: Select the most appropriate answer for each of the following questions regarding the quality controls of critical thinking.

### Questions

1. Which standard is considered the "Gateway Standard" because we cannot evaluate anything else until it is met?

- A) Accuracy
    
- B) Clarity
    
- C) Relevance
    
- D) Precision
    

2. If someone says, "Most domestic dogs weigh over 500 lbs," which intellectual standard is being most directly violated?

- A) Clarity
    
- B) Logic
    
- C) Accuracy
    
- D) Depth
    

3. Asking "Could you be more specific?" or "Could you give me more details?" is a check for which standard?

- A) Precision
    
- B) Breadth
    
- C) Logic
    
- D) Depth
    

4. Which standard asks: "How does that relate to the problem we are trying to solve?"

- A) Significance
    
- B) Relevance
    
- C) Breadth
    
- D) Clarity
    

5. When a solution or statement only addresses the surface level of a complex, multi-layered issue, it lacks:

- A) Breadth
    
- B) Logic
    
- C) Accuracy
    
- D) Depth
    

6. If a line of reasoning is deep and detailed but only considers one side of a multi-dimensional argument, it lacks:

- A) Breadth
    
- B) Precision
    
- C) Significance
    
- D) Accuracy
    

7. Which standard ensures that our conclusions follow consistently from the evidence provided without contradictions?

- B) Logic
    
- B) Fairness
    
- C) Depth
    
- D) Relevance
    

8. Asking "Is this the most important fact to consider, or are we focusing on a minor detail?" helps verify:

- A) Precision
    
- B) Significance
    
- C) Fairness
    
- D) Clarity
    

9. Representing viewpoints you disagree with accurately, sympathetically, and without bias is an act of:

- A) Logic
    
- B) Depth
    
- C) Fairness
    
- D) Accuracy
    

10. True or False: A statement can be clear, accurate, and precise, yet still fail to be relevant or deep.

- A) True
    
- B) False
    

  
  
  
  
  
  

### Answer Key & Explanations

  

1. B (Clarity): Clarity is the gateway; if a statement is unclear, we cannot determine if it is accurate or relevant.
    
2. C (Accuracy): Accuracy is the standard of verifiable truth. While the statement is clear, it is factually incorrect.
    
3. A (Precision): Precision requires providing enough details to understand exactly what is meant.
    
4. B (Relevance): Relevance ensures that the reasoning bears directly upon the question at issue.
    
5. D (Depth): Depth is the standard used to address the complexities and difficulties of an issue.
    
6. A (Breadth): Breadth requires looking at an issue from multiple perspectives or points of view.
    
7. A (Logic): Logic ensures that thoughts are mutually supporting and make sense together.
    
8. B (Significance): Significance focuses the mind on the most important information and central ideas.
    
9. C (Fairness): Fairness requires representing all viewpoints sympathetically and without vested interest.
    
10. A (True): Reasoning can pass some standards while failing others. Excellent thought requires meeting all nine standards.
